# 👨‍🏫 Teacher Handbook: Setting & Grading Exams

Welcome to your **Teacher Dashboard** on ExamNova! As an educator, your tools are focused on creating seamless assessments, setting schedules, and effortlessly compiling grades for your students.

This simple, step-by-step guide will help you navigate the system with complete confidence.

<br/>

## 🖨️ Phase 1: The Printing Room (Questions)
This is where the magic begins. You start by drafting your examination questions without worrying about scheduling them yet.

1. **Create a Question Paper**  
   Navigate to **Question Papers** and click the *"Create New Module"* button. Give it a clear name like *"Basic Science Mid-Term"*.
   
2. **Type Out Your Questions**  
   Add questions inside your module. You can mix and match formats effortlessly—Multiple Choice, True/False, or Essay questions.

> **💡 Pro Tip:**  
> Once created, **Question Papers are permanent**. This means you can easily reuse the exact same questions for different classes or future terms!

<br/>

## 📁 Phase 2: Exam Bundles & Folders
After drafting your question papers, you need to bundle them up into the actual exam that students will sit for.

1. **Create an Exam Bundle**  
   Go to **Exam Bundles** and click *"Create New Bundle"* (for example, *"JSS 1 First Term Examinations"*).
   
2. **Pack the Folder**  
   Select the Question Papers you created earlier and drop them into this bundle. This lets you combine multiple topics into one unified exam session if you wish!

<br/>

## 📅 Phase 3: The Timetable
Now that the exam is packaged, it is time to schedule when the students can take it.

1. **Set the Schedule**  
   Go to **Exam Schedules** and click *"Create New Schedule"*. Find the Exam Bundle you just prepared.
   
2. **Select Candidates**  
   Choose exactly *who* will be sitting for this exam. You can pick an entire **Class** or just individual **Students**.
   
3. **Set the Clock**  
   Define the exact **Date**, the **Starting Time**, and the **Duration**.

> **👀 What Happens Next?**  
> You're done! The exam will automatically pop up on the students' dashboards at the exact time you scheduled. 

<br/>

## 📑 Phase 4: The Result Office (Report Cards)
When exams are over, compiling scores is automatic and stress-free. Navigate to **Report Cards** from the sidebar to begin.

### Step 1: Select Your Context
Before you can view or enter scores, you must select the correct **filter context** at the top of the page:

| Filter       | What to choose                                  |
|--------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Session**  | The academic year (e.g. *2025/2026*)            |
| **Term**     | The specific term (e.g. *First Term*)           |
| **Class**    | The class you teach (e.g. *JSS 1A*)            |
| **Subject**  | Your subject (e.g. *Mathematics*)              |

Once all four filters are selected, click **"Fetch Students"** to load the class list.

### Step 2: Add or Sync Scores
You have **two ways** to get scores into the system:

- **🔄 Sync Online Scores** *(Recommended for CBT exams)*  
  Click the green **"Sync Online Scores"** button. The system will automatically pull in the exam marks from any computer-based tests the students completed online. Objective questions are marked instantly!

- **✏️ Manual Entry** *(For handwritten tests, practicals, or CA)*  
  Use the score grid that appears after fetching students. Each row has two input fields:
  - **Test Score** (out of 40) — Enter Continuous Assessment marks here.
  - **Exam Score** (out of 60) — Enter the final examination mark.
  
  The **Total** column updates automatically.  
  When you're done, click the green **"Save Scores"** button.

- **📤 Bulk Upload CSV**  
  For large classes, click the blue **"Bulk Upload CSV"** button. Prepare a spreadsheet with student IDs and their scores, then upload it in one go.

### Step 3: Grant Extra Time (During Live Exams)
If a student encounters technical issues during a live online exam, you can grant them more time without disrupting anyone else.

- **For one student:** Click the yellow **"Extra Time ⏳"** button on that student's row. Enter the number of extra minutes (e.g. 15).
- **For the whole class:** Click the **"Bulk Extra Time ⏳"** button at the top of the score sheet. This grants extra minutes to *every* student currently taking the exam.

> **⚠️ Important:**  
> Extra Time only works while the exam is still active. Once the exam window has closed, this option has no effect.

### Step 4: Review & Finalize
After all scores are entered:

1. Verify each student's **Test** and **Exam** scores are correct.
2. Use the **"Edit Details"** button on any row to view and fine-tune individual report card data.
3. Use the **"Print Report"** button to generate a printable report card for a student.
4. Use the **"Lock 🔒 / Unlock 🔓"** toggle to control whether parents can see a specific student's result.

### Step 5: Submission & Moderation
Results entered by Teachers are initially stored as **"Drafts"**. 

- **Drafting:** As you save scores, the status remains in local draft mode.
- **Submission:** Once you are satisfied, the scores are available to the **Manager** for moderation.
- **Approval:** A Manager or Head Teacher must review and **Approve/Publish** the results before they become official and visible to parents in the student portal.

### Step 6: View the Broadsheet
Click the purple **"Class Broadsheet"** button to see a bird's-eye view of the entire class's performance across all subjects. This is perfect for staff meetings and academic reviews.

> **🎉 Congratulations!**  
> You just managed an entire examination lifecycle — from drafting questions to publishing final results — without touching a physical calculator or ledger.
